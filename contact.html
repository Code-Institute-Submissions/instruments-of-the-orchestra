What is an Orchestra?

An orchestra is a large group of musicians who play instruments together to make music.

An orchestra typically plays a lot of classical music as well music used in films, television shows and video games!

The instruments in an orchestra are organised into four different families:

The String Family

The Woodwind Family

The Brass Family

The Percussion Family

An orchestra can have between 30 and 100 musicians playing on stage at the same time
...that’s a lot of musicians!

So how do all the musicians play together?

They are lead by
‘The Conductor’.

It is the conductors job to tell the musicians when to start and stop playing, how fast or slow to play, and how loud or quiet to play.
